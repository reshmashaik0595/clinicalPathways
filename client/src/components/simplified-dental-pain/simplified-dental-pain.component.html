Dental Pain (Odontalgia)

  • Most dental pain can be managed with referral to dentist + analgesia

    • Sinusitis
    • Cluster headaches
    • TMJ dysfunction
    • Trigeminal neuralgia
    • Herpes zoster
    • Aphthous ulcer
    • Parotiditis/sialolith

    • Pain exacerbated by cold or osmotic stimuli (i.e: sweets or acids)
    • Pain with biting with/without visible swelling of gums
    • Chronic pain
    • dentist may choose to give antibiotics at time of treatment, but not necessary to give in primary care pre-referral

    • Tooth extremely tender when palpated or tapped + any of the following:
      • Severe spontaneous pain, relieved by cold & exacerbated by heat
      • Night pain (pain worse when supine)
      • Mild/moderate facial swelling +/- fever
    • If severely immunocompromised, this may lower the threshold for antibiotics and increase the urgency to see a dentist
    • If unable to see dentist within a reasonable time this may also lower the threshold for antibiotics, use clinical judgment

  • Red Flags?

    • Please note all medication doses but be corrected for renal function.

    • Ibuprofen 400 mg PO QID prn
      • NSAIDs shown to be more effective in reducing pain than opioid analgesics and have been recommended as first line
    • Tylenol 1000 mg PO QID prn
    • Topical Local Analgesics
      • Orajel - useful for gums/lacerations/ulcers
    • Opioids (i.e: Tylenol #3) May be considered for short term use only in severe acute pain, or if intolerance of NSAIDs

    • Recommended Antibiotics
      • Amoxicillin 500 mg PO TID x 5 days
        • If first line treatment fails:
          • Add metronidazole 500 mg PO TID x 7 days OR
          • Discontinue Amoxicillin and Start Amoxicillin and Clavulanate 500/125 mg TID x 7 days
      • Allergy to penicillin
        • Clindamycin 300 mg PO QID x 3-7 days
        • Cephalexin 500 mg PO QID x 3-7 days
        • If treatment fails:
          • Add metronidazole 500 mg PO TID x 7 days
